The Great Irish Potato Famine

Overview

The Great Irish Potato Famine was a period of mass starvation, disease, and emigration in Ireland between 1845 and 1852.

Causes

The famine was caused by the water mold disease known as late blight, which resulted in crop failure three years in a row. This was exacerbated by political factors and economic ideology.

Effects

The famine caused the deaths of about one million people and the emigration of another two million people.
